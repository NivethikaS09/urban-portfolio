Regional

Mitigating Human–Elephant Conflict Through Sustainable Wild Elephant Tourism in Sri Lanka

Mapped Scope National / Sri Lanka
Analysis Period 2025
Primary Sensors Stakeholder Interviews, Literature Review, Policy Audits

Analysis Framework

Conducted a research study exploring how sustainable wildlife tourism can reduce human–elephant conflict (HEC) while improving local livelihoods and supporting elephant conservation. The study proposed community-based tourism models, diversified tourism experiences, and policy interventions to create a balanced approach to economic development and biodiversity conservation.

Research Exhibits & Analysis

Exhibit 1: HEC Mapping
Exhibit 2: CBT Model Flow
Exhibit 3: Field Observation

Key Outcomes & Findings

  • Identified the limitations of Sri Lanka's current wildlife tourism model, which relies heavily on jeep safaris.
  • Demonstrated how community-based tourism can generate alternative livelihoods while reducing dependence on agriculture in HEC-prone areas.
  • Proposed diversified tourism activities such as river safaris, guided nature walks, eco-lodges, and observation platforms to improve visitor experiences and reduce environmental impacts.
  • Recommended sustainable tourism policies including visitor carrying capacities, designated wildlife viewing zones, eco-certification, and conservation-focused tourism management.
  • Highlighted the importance of local community participation, education, and revenue-sharing in achieving long-term wildlife conservation and economic resilience.
  • Developed planning recommendations to balance tourism growth, biodiversity conservation, and community development in Sri Lanka.

Land Use/Land Cover Classification of Dambulla DSD Using Remote Sensing

Mapped Scope Dambulla DSD
Analysis Period 2025
Primary Sensors Landsat 7 Satellite Imagery

Applied remote sensing techniques to classify land cover using Landsat 7 multispectral imagery. Both ISO Cluster (Unsupervised) and Maximum Likelihood (Supervised) classification approaches were implemented, followed by raster refinement and polygon conversion to generate planning-ready land use maps.
