Colombo

Air Quality Modelling and PM2.5 Analysis for Colombo Using Python

Mapped Scope Colombo Municipal Area
Analysis Period 2018 Historical Data
Primary Sensors Air Quality Monitoring Station Records

Analysis Framework

Developed a Python-based air quality modelling workflow to analyze hourly and daily PM2.5 concentrations in Colombo using 2018 historical air quality data. The project involved data preprocessing, statistical analysis, time-series visualization, and trend interpretation to understand urban air pollution patterns and support environmental planning decisions.

Key Outcomes & Findings

  • Developed a complete workflow for retrieving, cleaning, and analyzing 2018 PM2.5 air quality data for Colombo.
  • Identified significant temporal fluctuations in PM2.5 concentrations, with several extreme pollution events exceeding 500 µg/m³.
  • Performed exploratory data analysis (EDA) and generated hourly and daily PM2.5 trend visualizations.
  • Applied time-series resampling to produce daily average pollutant concentrations for improved trend analysis.
  • Highlighted vehicular traffic, industrial activities, construction dust, and biomass burning as the dominant contributors to Colombo's air pollution.
  • Demonstrated how data-driven air quality modelling can support pollution hotspot identification and evidence-based environmental planning.

Technologies & Analysis

Python Google Colab Time Series Analysis Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) Data Preprocessing PM2.5 Air Quality Analysis Environmental Modelling
